How Craps Works

Craps is built around a two-phase system. Start with the Pass Line bet — once you get that, everything else makes sense.

Phase 1 — Come-Out Roll

  • Roll 7 or 11 → Natural! Pass Line wins immediately 🎉
  • Roll 2, 3, or 12 → Craps! Pass Line loses
  • Roll 4,5,6,8,9, or 10 → That number becomes the POINT. The puck on the table flips to ON.

Phase 2 — Point Phase

  • Roll the point again → Pass Line wins! New come-out starts.
  • Roll a 7 → "Seven out." Pass Line loses. Dice pass to new shooter.
  • All other numbers → keep rolling, no Pass Line result yet.
💡 Vegas Tip

The whole table usually bets together. When the shooter hits the point, everyone wins together — that energy is why craps is the most exciting game on the floor!

Pass Line

Pass Line1.41% edge ★★★

Win on 7/11 come-out. Lose on 2/3/12. In point phase, win if point rolls before 7. Pays 1:1.

Odds (behind Pass)0% edge ★★★★

The only 0% edge bet in any casino. After point is set, tap ODDS. Pays 2:1 on 4/10, 3:2 on 5/9, 6:5 on 6/8.

Don't Pass Bar1.36% edge ★★★

Betting against the shooter. Better odds but you're rooting against the table. Win on 2/3, push on 12, lose on 7/11.

Place Bets

Place 6 or 81.52% edge ★★★

Bet that 6 (or 8) rolls before 7. Stays active. Pays 7:6. Best place bets on the table.

Place 5 or 94.0% edge ★★☆

Pays 7:5. Acceptable.

Place 4 or 106.67% edge ✗

Pays 9:5. High edge — avoid or buy.

One-Roll & Prop Bets

Field5.5% edge ★☆

Wins on 2,3,4,9,10,11,12. Pays 1:1, except 2&12 pay 2:1. Loses on 5,6,7,8.

Any 716.7% edge ✗✗✗

Pays 4:1. True odds are 5:1. Worst bet on the table. Avoid.

Hardways9–11% edge ✗✗

Win if number rolls as doubles (3+3 = Hard 6) before a 7 or easy way. Hard 6/8 pay 9:1, Hard 4/10 pay 7:1.

Step-by-Step Beginner Strategy

  • Bet Pass Line every come-out
  • After point is set, tap ODDS — free odds has 0% house edge
  • Add Place 6 and Place 8 if they're not already the point
  • That's it — you're playing with the best odds possible

The Math — Why 6 and 8?

  • 7: 6 ways to roll — most common number
  • 6 and 8: 5 ways each — 2nd most common
  • 5 and 9: 4 ways each
  • 4 and 10: 3 ways each — hardest to hit

Bankroll Rules

  • Set a hard loss limit before you sit down
  • $100–$200 is a solid session for a $5 table
  • If you're up 50%, pocket half and play with profits
  • Never raise bets to chase losses
⚠️ Gambler's Fallacy

Past rolls don't affect future rolls. A cold table doesn't mean a hot streak is due. Every roll is independent.

At the Table

  • Buy in between rolls — place cash on table, say "Change, please"
  • Keep hands out of the table when dice are in the air
  • Pass Line and Field are self-service; other bets go through dealers
  • Have your bet ready — don't hold up the game

As the Shooter

  • Use one hand only to throw the dice
  • Both dice must bounce off the far wall
  • Never say "seven" at the table during the point phase — say "big red" if you must reference it

Tipping & Lingo

  • Tip after a good run — dealers work for tips. Bet a chip for them on the Pass Line: "For the dealers!"
  • "Coming out" — starting a new round
  • "Seven out" — 7 rolled during point phase, round ends
  • "Color me up" — exchange small chips for large ones when leaving
  • "Press it" — double a winning bet
🎰 Last Tip

Tell the dealer you're new — most will guide you. Buy in small, just bet the Pass Line at first, and soak in the atmosphere. Craps is the most social game in Vegas!
