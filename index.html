How Craps Works

Craps looks chaotic at first, but it's actually built around a simple two-phase structure. Once you understand that, everything else clicks into place.

Phase 1: The Come-Out Roll

Every round starts with a come-out roll. If you have a Pass Line bet (the most popular bet at the table):

  • Roll 7 or 11 — Win immediately! This is a "Natural."
  • Roll 2, 3, or 12 — Lose immediately. This is "Craps."
  • Any other number (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10) — That becomes the point. The puck flips to ON and the game moves to Phase 2.

Phase 2: The Point Phase

Once the point is set, the shooter keeps rolling until one of two things happens:

  • The point number is rolled again — Pass Line wins! Round resets to a new come-out roll.
  • A 7 is rolled — "Seven out." Pass Line loses. Dice pass to a new shooter.
💡 Vegas Tip

In a live casino, the whole table usually bets with the shooter. When the shooter hits the point, everyone wins together — that communal energy is what makes craps the most exciting table game in the casino!

The Table Layout

The craps table is divided into zones for different bets. The most important ones are at the bottom (Pass Line) and the numbered boxes in the middle. Don't let the busy layout intimidate you — just focus on the Pass Line to start.

All the Bets Explained

Pass Line
1.41% House Edge ★★★

The most fundamental craps bet. You're betting with the shooter.

  • Come-out roll: Win on 7 or 11, lose on 2/3/12
  • Point phase: Win if the point rolls before a 7
  • Payout: 1:1
Don't Pass Bar
1.36% House Edge ★★★

Opposite of Pass Line — you're betting against the shooter. Lower house edge, but at a real casino, don't celebrate when the table loses!

  • Come-out roll: Win on 2 or 3, lose on 7/11, push (tie) on 12
  • Point phase: Win if a 7 rolls before the point
  • Payout: 1:1
Come Bet
1.41% House Edge ★★★

Exactly like the Pass Line, but placed after a point is established. The next roll acts as your personal come-out roll. Available only during the point phase.

  • Next roll: Win on 7/11, lose on 2/3/12
  • Any other number: that becomes your come-point
  • Payout: 1:1
Place Bets (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10)
1.5%–6.7% Edge ★★☆

Bet that a specific number will roll before a 7. These stay up until you win or a 7 is rolled. Best placed on 6 and 8.

  • Place 6 or 8: 7:6 — House edge: 1.52%
  • Place 5 or 9: 7:5 — House edge: 4.0%
  • Place 4 or 10: 9:5 — House edge: 6.67%
Field Bet
5.5% House Edge ★☆☆

A one-roll bet. Win if the next roll is 2, 3, 4, 9, 10, 11, or 12. Lose on 5, 6, 7, or 8.

  • 3, 4, 9, 10, 11: 1:1
  • 2 or 12: 2:1
  • Losing numbers: 5, 6, 7, 8
Any 7
16.7% House Edge ✗✗✗

One-roll bet that the next roll is a 7. High payout but terrible odds.

  • Payout: 4:1 — True odds are 5:1
⚠️ Sucker Bet

Over 16% house edge. The casino loves when tourists play this. Avoid it entirely.

Any Craps
11.1% House Edge ✗✗

One-roll bet that the next roll is 2, 3, or 12. Sometimes used to hedge a Pass Line bet on the come-out.

  • Payout: 7:1

The Best Bets for Beginners

Craps has some of the best and the worst bets in any casino. Knowing the difference is the most valuable thing you can learn before your trip.

🏆 Beginner's Core Strategy

Stick to Pass Line + Place 6 & 8. These three bets have very low house edges, are simple to manage, and keep you involved in nearly every roll.

Bet Rankings: Best to Worst

★★★ Don't Pass / Don't Come — 1.36% edge (lowest on the table, but betting against the table is socially awkward)
★★★ Pass Line / Come — 1.41% edge (the classic, most popular bet. Start here.)
★★☆ Place 6 or Place 8 — 1.52% edge (great secondary bets once a point is set)
★☆☆ Place 5 or Place 9 — 4.0% edge (acceptable, not ideal)
✗✗ Field Bet — 5.5% edge (it looks like a lot of numbers win but the math is against you)
✗✗✗ Any 7, Hardways, Prop Bets — 9–16% edge (avoid entirely)

Taking Free Odds — The Best Bet in the Casino

After making a Pass Line bet and a point is set, you can make an additional "Free Odds" bet behind your Pass Line chips. This is the only bet in any casino with zero house edge — the casino pays true mathematical odds.

  • Point is 4 or 10: Odds pay 2:1
  • Point is 5 or 9: Odds pay 3:2
  • Point is 6 or 8: Odds pay 6:5
💡 Pro Tip

Most Vegas casinos allow 3x–4x–5x odds (3x on 4/10, 4x on 5/9, 5x on 6/8). Always take the max odds allowed — it's free money in the long run relative to any other bet.

Craps Strategy

Beginner Strategy: "Pass Line + Place 6 & 8"

  • Always bet the Pass Line before the come-out roll
  • Once a point is set, place $6 on both 6 and 8 (if one of them isn't already the point)
  • When you win on 6 or 8, you can collect the winnings or "press" (add to) the bet
  • You're now covered on three of the six most-rolled numbers

The Math: Why 6 and 8 Are Great

There are 36 possible dice combinations. Here's how often key numbers appear:

  • 7: 6 ways — most common roll (1+6, 2+5, 3+4, 4+3, 5+2, 6+1)
  • 6: 5 ways — second most common (1+5, 2+4, 3+3, 4+2, 5+1)
  • 8: 5 ways — tied for second (2+6, 3+5, 4+4, 5+3, 6+2)
  • 5 & 9: 4 ways each
  • 4 & 10: 3 ways each — hardest to hit

Bankroll Management

  • Set a hard stop-loss before you sit down — if you lose it, walk away
  • A reasonable session bankroll for $5 craps: $100–$200
  • Never bet more than 10% of your session bankroll on a single roll
  • If you're up 50% or more, consider pocketing half your profit and playing with the rest
  • Remember: the house always has an edge. Play for fun, not to make money.
⚠️ Gambler's Fallacy

Past rolls have zero effect on future rolls. If the table has been "cold" (lots of seven-outs), the next roll still has the exact same odds. There are no "due" numbers. Each roll is completely independent.

Volatility & Streaks

Craps is a volatile game. A hot shooter can multiply your stack in minutes. A cold session can wipe you out just as fast. The house edge is low per bet, but the pace of the game means many bets are resolved quickly. Pace yourself, enjoy the social atmosphere, and play within your means.

Casino Craps Etiquette

Craps has more unwritten rules than almost any other casino game. Follow these and you'll fit right in with the regulars.

At the Table

  • Buy in between rolls, never while the dice are out — place cash on the table and say "Change, please"
  • Keep your hands out of the table when the dice are in the air
  • Don't reach across the table — if you can't reach a bet, ask the dealer
  • Pass Line bets are self-service; most other bets go through the dealers

As the Shooter

  • Use one hand only to pick up and throw the dice
  • Always hit the far wall of the table — both dice must bounce off it
  • Don't take forever choosing dice or shaking them — keep the game moving
  • Never say the word "seven" at the table during a point phase — it's considered terrible luck. Say "big red" instead if you must reference it.

Tipping the Dealers

  • Dealers work mostly for tips — it's customary to tip after a good run
  • A fun way to tip: place a chip for the dealers on the Pass Line ("This one's for the crew!")
  • $5–$10 per session is normal for low-to-mid stakes games

Table Lingo to Know

  • "Coming out" — starting a new round
  • "Seven out" — a 7 rolled during the point phase, ending the round
  • "Color me up" — say this when leaving to exchange small chips for larger ones
  • "Press it" — double a winning bet
  • "Working" — your bets are active on this roll
  • "Off" — temporarily disabling your bets for a roll
🎰 Final Tip

Craps dealers genuinely enjoy helping newcomers. If you're nervous, just tell them — most will be happy to guide you through your first few bets. Buy in for a modest amount, start with just the Pass Line, and soak in the energy of the table. It's one of the most fun experiences in Vegas!
